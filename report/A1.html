Introduction

The dataset analyses customer behavior using a marketing dataset. In the dataset, there are variables for marketing spend, visits, purchase amount, and segment for each customer. With this information we can understand whether the marketing spend on customers has positive effects.

Research Question section

This report aims to know the relationship between marketing spend and purchase amount, and whether high marketing spend will cause more visits.

This dataset contains customer information on marketing activities and purchasing behavior. It includes customer id, signup date, marketing spend, visits, purchase amount, segment, and notes. The dataset is used to analyse whether marketing spend has positive effects on customer behavior.

Before the analysis, a few cleaning steps were done. First, the report only needs marketing_spend, purchase_amount, segment, and visits, so the other columns were removed as they were not relevant to the analysis. Moreover, rows with NA values were removed to increase the quality of the analysis.

Results section

Figure 2 shows a positive relationship between marketing spend and purchase amount across all three segments. The correlation between marketing spend and purchase amount is 0.83, indicating a strong positive association. As marketing spend increases, purchase amount also tends to increase for Consumer, Enterprise, and SMB customers.

Figure 3 shows a positive relationship between marketing spend and visits. The correlation between marketing spend and visits is 0.42, indicating a positive but weak association. To sum up, customers with higher marketing spend tend to have more visits. However, the wide spread of the points suggest that there may be other factors affecting customer visits.
